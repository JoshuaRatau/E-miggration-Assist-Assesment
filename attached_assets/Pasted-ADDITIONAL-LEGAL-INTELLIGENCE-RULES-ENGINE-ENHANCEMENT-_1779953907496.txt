ADDITIONAL LEGAL INTELLIGENCE & RULES ENGINE ENHANCEMENT

---

## ASSESSMENT TITLE & POSITIONING

The title of the experience must immediately and clearly communicate that this assessment specifically relates to immigration overstays, undesirable declarations, prohibition-related concerns, and immigration status complications in South Africa.

Recommended title:

“South African Overstay & Undesirable Status Preliminary Assessment”

Alternative:
“Out-of-Status Immigration Assessment”

Subtitle:
“Answer a few guided questions to better understand your current immigration circumstances and possible next procedural considerations.”

Important explanatory text directly below the title:

“This guided assessment is designed to help identify and organise information relating to possible overstays, undesirable declarations, immigration status complications, and related circumstances under South African immigration processes.”

“This assessment generates a preliminary informational report only and does not constitute legal advice, legal representation, or a formal immigration opinion.”

“This tool is intended to assist with understanding possible next-step considerations based on the information provided.”

---

## LEGAL & REGULATORY INTELLIGENCE ENGINE

The system must incorporate a rule-based immigration logic engine informed by South African immigration laws, regulations, directives, and operational procedures relating to:

• visa overstays,
• undesirable declarations,
• immigration prohibition,
• administrative offences,
• pending applications,
• waiver scenarios,
• appeal eligibility,
• overstay duration,
• and procedural risk indicators.

The rules engine should be modular and scalable for future legal updates.

---

## LEGAL SOURCES TO INFORM RULES ENGINE

The logic engine must be informed by:

1. Immigration Act 13 of 2002
2. Immigration Amendment Act 13 of 2011
3. Immigration Regulations, 2014
4. Section 30(1)(h) — undesirable persons
5. Regulation 27(3) — overstay periods and undesirable durations
6. Section 50(1) — administrative offences
7. DHA operational directives
8. DHA overstay appeal procedures
9. DHA Form 19 undesirable declaration framework
10. Pending application concession practices where applicable
11. Waiver and representation procedural practices
12. Relevant DHA notices and concession extensions where applicable

---

## RULE-BASED SCENARIO LOGIC

The system must dynamically generate preliminary insight outputs based on user responses.

IMPORTANT:
The system must NOT provide definitive legal advice or legal outcomes.

Instead:
it should generate:
• procedural indicators,
• risk observations,
• possible considerations,
• and suggested next-step themes.

---

## SCENARIO VARIABLES

The rules engine must analyse variables including:

• inside or outside South Africa
• overstay duration
• prior overstay history
• undesirable declaration issued or not
• pending application before expiry
• pending waiver
• pending appeal
• rejection received
• proof of submission available
• medical/family emergency factors
• inability to depart
• administrative delay indicators
• legal representation status
• uploaded supporting documents
• travel restriction concerns
• urgency level
• level of uncertainty/confusion

## PRELIMINARY RULE EXAMPLES

RULE EXAMPLE 1

IF:
• overstay <= 30 days
AND
• undesirable declaration received

THEN:
display informational insight:

“Based on the information provided, your situation may relate to a shorter-duration overstay scenario under South African immigration regulations.”

“Certain overstay declarations may be subject to procedural representations or review processes depending on the surrounding circumstances.”

---

RULE EXAMPLE 2

IF:
• overstay > 30 days
AND
• outside South Africa

THEN:
display:

“The information provided may indicate a longer-duration overstay circumstance that could affect future immigration applications or re-entry processes.”

“Additional procedural review or formal immigration assistance may be required.”

---

RULE EXAMPLE 3

IF:
• application submitted before expiry
AND
• acknowledgement receipt available

THEN:
display:

“The information provided suggests that an immigration application may have been submitted before visa expiry.”

“In certain circumstances, supporting documentation and procedural records may become important when assessing immigration status complications.”

---

RULE EXAMPLE 4

IF:
• medical emergency selected

THEN:
display:

“Medical or humanitarian circumstances are often important supporting considerations in immigration-related representations and procedural reviews.”

RULE EXAMPLE 5

IF:
• user indicates uncertainty regarding current status

THEN:
display:

“Many individuals experience uncertainty regarding immigration status processes, especially where delays, pending outcomes, or administrative complications exist.”

This report must:
• summarise the information provided,
• organise key immigration indicators,
• identify possible procedural themes,
• indicate potential risk factors,
• and suggest practical next-step categories.

The report should feel:
• intelligent,
• reassuring,
• organised,
• and highly professional.
