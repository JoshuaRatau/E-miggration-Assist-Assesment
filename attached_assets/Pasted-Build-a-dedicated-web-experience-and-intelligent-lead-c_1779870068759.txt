Build a dedicated web experience and intelligent lead-capture workflow specifically for individuals who may have overstayed in South Africa or who have been declared undesirable following an overstay.

This experience forms part of the E-Migration Assist ecosystem and must feel:
• calm,
• reassuring,
• intelligent,
• legally aware,
• mobile-first,
• modern,
• and emotionally safe for the visitor.

The purpose of this experience is:

1. to help the visitor better understand their situation through guided questions,
2. to reduce panic and uncertainty,
3. to gather structured lead intelligence,
4. to determine the nature of the overstay circumstance,
5. and to route the lead directly into the Lead Intelligence Dashboard for engagement and conversion workflows.

IMPORTANT:
This is NOT merely a generic contact form.
It must behave like a guided immigration triage and qualification experience.

---

## LEGAL & PRACTICAL CONTEXT

The workflow and wording should align with South African immigration overstay realities under the Immigration Act and Department of Home Affairs practices.

The system should recognise that overstays commonly arise from:
• visa expiry,
• delayed waiver outcomes,
• rejected applications,
• inability to depart,
• misunderstandings regarding legal status,
• pending applications,
• financial hardship,
• medical/family emergencies,
• or administrative delays.

The UX must therefore avoid:
• aggressive legal wording,
• threatening terminology,
• or panic-inducing visuals.

Instead:
the interface should position itself as:
“helping the visitor understand the next practical steps.”

No legal conclusions must be automatically given.
This is a preliminary guided intake and lead qualification process only.

---

## INITIAL LANDING EXPERIENCE

When the user lands on the page:

Display a modern hero section with:
• reassuring headline,
• calm professional design,
• subtle animated background,
• and clear call-to-action.

Suggested tone:
“You may still have options available.”

Suggested supporting text:
“If you have overstayed in South Africa or received an undesirable declaration, this guided assessment may help you better understand your circumstances and possible next steps.”

The page must immediately feel:
• trustworthy,
• premium,
• structured,
• and confidential.

Use the official E-Migration Assist brand styling and colour palette.

---

## STEP 1 — NAME CAPTURE & PERSONALISATION

The first interaction should ask ONLY one question initially:

“What is your first name?”

Once entered:
the interface should dynamically personalise the experience.

Example:
“Thank you, Sarah.”
“Let’s better understand your situation.”

The experience must feel conversational and human.

---

## STEP 2 — GUIDED IMMIGRATION TRIAGE QUESTIONS

The form must progressively reveal questions one-by-one.

Do NOT display all questions simultaneously.

The interaction should resemble:
• intelligent onboarding,
• legal-tech workflow guidance,
• or conversational assessment logic.

Questions should adapt based on previous responses.

---

## RECOMMENDED QUESTION FLOW

1. What best describes your current situation?

Options:
• My visa has expired
• I received an undesirable declaration
• I overstayed after my visa expiry
• I am unsure about my current legal status
• My application was rejected while I remained in South Africa
• I missed my departure deadline
• Other

---

2. Are you currently inside or outside South Africa?

Options:
• Inside South Africa
• Outside South Africa

---

3. Approximately how long have you overstayed?

Options:
• Less than 30 days
• 30 days to 90 days
• More than 90 days
• Unsure

---

4. Did you attempt to submit any application before your visa expired?

Options:
• Yes
• No
• Unsure

IF YES:
show follow-up:
“What type of application did you submit?”

---

5. Have you received any written communication from the Department of Home Affairs?

Options:
• Yes
• No
• Unsure

IF YES:
allow upload field for:
• undesirable declaration,
• rejection letter,
• passport stamp,
• visa copy,
• VFS communication,
• or related document.

---

6. What has been your biggest challenge so far?

Multi-select:
• Understanding what to do next
• Fear of being banned
• Difficulty communicating with authorities
• Financial constraints
• Delays and uncertainty
• Lack of legal guidance
• Stress/anxiety regarding status
• Travel restrictions
• Other

---

7. What type of assistance are you looking for?

Options:
• Understanding my legal position
• Overstay appeal assistance
• Guidance on possible next steps
• Professional immigration support
• Future visa planning
• General immigration guidance

## FINAL SCREEN EXPERIENCE

At the end:
display a structured preliminary outcome message.

DO NOT provide legal advice or legal conclusions.

Instead show:

“Thank you, [Name].”

“Based on the information provided, your situation may require a structured review and guided next-step support.”

Then explain:
• their information has been securely captured,
• a preliminary reference number has been generated,
• and they may soon be invited to continue onboarding into the E-Migration Assist platform.

Generate:
Unique reference number format:
EMA-OVR-2026-XXXX

## LEAD INTELLIGENCE DASHBOARD INTEGRATION

ALL captured data must automatically push into the Lead Intelligence Dashboard.

The system must:
• create lead profile,
• classify as:
“Overstay / Undesirable Lead”
• assign lead score,
• assign source attribution,
• log timestamp,
• and route into the correct pipeline automatically.

---

## LEAD SCORING LOGIC

Suggested scoring:
• completed assessment = +20
• uploaded supporting documents = +20
• requested assistance = +15
• WhatsApp opt-in = +10
• outside South Africa = +10
• overstay > 90 days = +15
• indicated stress/fear concerns = +5

If score >= 50:
route to:
“High Priority Immigration Follow-Up”
